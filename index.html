WELCOME TO MY E-PORTFOLIO

About me

I am Harsh Sharma, a student in the Early Childhood Education program at Conestoga College, preparing for a career as an early childhood educator. My passion for supporting young children's learning and development inspired me to pursue this field. This e-portfolio highlights my experiences and reflections during field placements, including classroom observations and insights into child development and early education practices. It also includes my CPR and First Aid certifications, demonstrating my commitment to ensuring a safe and nurturing environment for children. Through coursework, practical sessions, and classroom visits, I am developing the skills necessary to create secure, inclusive, and supportive learning environments. My goal is to positively impact the lives of children and their families by fostering environments where children feel safe, valued, and ready to thrive.

Education Qualifications

In preparation of my experience of the field placement, I have and am still attending in-class activities where I will be observing and learning about child development, management of the behavior of the child and early learning techniques. It is in these sessions that I have noted:

  1. The way teachers communicate with the children with positive words.
  2. Routine activities such as snack breaks, circle time and going outside Learning at an appropriate age by story telling, music and play
  3. Tactics of promoting cooperation and self control
  4. The value of healthy, clean and friendly place

These experiences are assisting me in having a solid ground to stand on before going to an actual placement environment.

Certificates

CPR and First Aid Certificates

I have also done and received my CPR (Level C) course and Standard First Aid qualifications as part of the requirements in the program. Such qualifications have empowered me with these abilities and confidence to react to emergencies and protect the safety of the children that I take care of.

Skills and Learning

I can reach the conclusion that though I have not yet been in a full placement, I have acquired some valuable building blocks of skills simply by observing and practicing in the classroom.

  • Child Observation: observation and deciphering of action and behavior. children & Safety Awareness: knowledge of procedures and coping with simulated emergency cases
  • Team Collaboration: collaborating with classmates to study the child development and classroom settings
  • CPR and First Aid: Qualified to deal with safety cases in real life.

    • Reflection

    The observation experiences that have been done in class are very helpful to me though I have not yet started my placement. They have made me learn to see children through a developmental perspective, identify their needs, and know how teachers help them develop. I am better able to enter the field with more confidence and preparation due to my coursework, my observations and my safety training.
